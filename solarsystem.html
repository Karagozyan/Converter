Solar System Planets

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is the smallest planet in our solar system. From the sun to Mercury is 69,8 milion km.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called the "Evening Star" or "Morning Star". From the sun to Venus is 108,9 milion km.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the only known planet to support life. From the sun to Earth is 152 milion km.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet and the furthest terrestrial planet from the Sun. From the sun to Mars is 249 milion km.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. From the sun to Jupiter is 816 milion km.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. From the sun to Saturn is 1,513 billion km.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is a gaseous cyan ice giant. From the sun to Uranus is 3.004 billion km.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the farthest known planet in the Solar System. From the sun to Neptune is 4,554 billion km.

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper belt, a ring of bodies beyond the orbit of Neptune. It is the ninth-largest and tenth-most-massive known object to directly orbit the Sun. From the sun to Uranus is 1446 Milion km.
